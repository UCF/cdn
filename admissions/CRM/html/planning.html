Planning for College

It’s not too early to start thinking about college. Why not, start planning for your future now. You may have lots of questions, including: What classes should I be taking for college? When do I apply to UCF? How can I afford college?

Here’s a checklist to make it easy to know what to do and when to do it. Hope it helps!

High School Courses
 
  • 4 years of college prep English
  • 4 years of Math (Algebra I and above)
  • 3 years of Science, 2 with a lab
  • 3 years of Social Studies
  • 2 years of the same foreign language
  • 2 years of academic electives

Junior Year
Early Junior year
  • Take PSAT
Late Junior year
  • Take SAT or ACT
  • Take a tour of UCF

Senior Year
Early Fall
  1. Apply to UCF
  2. Send high school transcript and test scores to UCF
  3. Search for scholarships
Late Fall
  1. Check your admission status
  2. Once admitted, apply for housing
  3. Retake SAT or ACT if needed
Winter
  1. Apply for financial aid and scholarships
  2. File your FAFSA
Spring
  1. Attend an Admitted Student Open House Event
  2. Submit enrollment deposit by May 1
  3. Send final high school transcripts to UCF
  4. Schedule and attend a UCF Orientation

You can be on your way to being a Knight sooner than you think—and we’re here to help you prepare. For more information about UCF’s requirements and application process, visit admissions.ucf.edu.
